Sampling Mean, Variance, and the Law of Large Numbers

Properties of Sampling Mean and Variance

The sampling mean (\(\bar{X}\)) and sampling variance (\(S^2\)) are crucial statistical measures with the following properties:

  • Sampling Mean (\(\bar{X}\)):
    • Unbiased: The expected value of the sample mean equals the population mean, \(E[\bar{X}] = \mu\).
    • Efficient: It is the most efficient estimator for the population mean under certain conditions.
    • Distribution: For sufficiently large sample sizes, \(\bar{X}\) follows a normal distribution due to the Central Limit Theorem.
  • Sampling Variance (\(S^2\)):
    • Unbiased: \(E[S^2] = \sigma^2\) for an unbiased estimator.
    • Measures the spread of data within a sample.

Law of Large Numbers

The Law of Large Numbers (LLN) states that as the size of a sample increases, the sample mean (\(\bar{X}\)) approaches the population mean (\(\mu\)).

Mathematical Formulation

        \[
        \lim_{n \to \infty} P(|\bar{X}_n - \mu| < \epsilon) = 1, \text{ for any } \epsilon > 0
        \]

This guarantees that larger samples provide more accurate estimates of population parameters.

Applications in Cybersecurity

The LLN has several practical applications in cybersecurity, including:

  • Intrusion Detection: Monitoring large datasets of network traffic to identify anomalies by estimating baseline behaviors.
  • Risk Assessment: Estimating the average time between security breaches to improve defensive strategies.
  • Randomness Testing: Evaluating the fairness of cryptographic random number generators over large samples.
